A Message of Welcome

The Synthetic Health Data Education and Training Masterclass Series brings together researchers, policymakers, data custodians, clinicians, and technical experts to build shared capability in the responsible use of synthetic health data. The series responds to growing demand for safe, ethical, and practical ways to accelerate data-driven health innovation while maintaining public trust.

Across the program, participants explore how synthetic data can be used to prototype analyses, test pipelines, and support workforce development without exposing sensitive real-world data. The masterclasses focus on understanding when synthetic data is appropriate, and how trade-offs between data utility and privacy can be navigated with confidence.

A strong emphasis is placed on governance, ethics, and social licence. Participants engage with contemporary governance frameworks, privacy-preserving analytics, and federated data ecosystem models that enable collaboration across institutions and jurisdictions.

Delivered in partnership with leading Australian universities and international experts from Swansea University and the SAIL Databank, the series is designed to strengthen cross-sector dialogue and equip participants with insights they can apply directly in research, policy, and service settings.

Program
Session Highlights
01
Synthetic Health Data Foundations & Governance

An introduction to what synthetic health data is (and is not), its appropriate use cases, and the governance, ethics, and social licence considerations required for responsible adoption.

Digital Health CRC leaders · University partners
02
Synthetic Health Data in Practice

A practical exploration of low- and high-fidelity synthetic data, informed by UK and Australian case studies and real-world implementations across health and research settings.

Swansea University · SAIL Databank · Australian collaborators
03
Privacy-Preserving Analytics & Federated Data Ecosystems

A deep dive into federated learning, trusted research environments, and collaboration models that enable analysis without centralising sensitive data across institutions and jurisdictions.

International & Australian technical experts
